Demand forecasting in automotive production

Material management data analysis for adaptive forecasting in the automotive industry under supply chain uncertainty through data-driven modeling and time series aggregation.

In recent years, the automotive manufacturing sector has faced unprecedented challenges due to global supply chain disruptions. Just in the year 2021 alone, the COVID-19 pandemic abruptly halted production and shifts in consumer global demand, a six-day blockage of the Suez Canal by the "Ever Given" in March delayed shipments for essential car components, and the widespread semiconductor shortages that crippled vehicle production well until 2023.

These events exposed how fragile the just-in-time manufacturing system could be, and the importance for forecasting models that could both assume a relatively stable time series and handle short-term disruptions which introduce extreme non-linearity, volatility and abrupt changes in otherwise "predictable" time series data.

Traditional forecasting models usually struggle to adapt in the face of unexpected schocks and rapid demand rebounds which often lead to flawed materials planning, inventory misalignment and production bottlenecks. Which highlights the need for more adaptive and resilient forecasting methodologies that can function in both short and long term analysis.

This project explores an advanced alternative: (Multiple Aggregation Prediction Algorithm (MAPA), proposed by Nikolaos Kourentzes in 2014. MAPA enhances forecasting accuracy by combining predictions across multiple levels of time aggregation, capturing both short-term fluctuations and medium-term trends.

By analyzing historical sales and supply chain signals in the automotive sector, this project demonstrates how MAPA can serve as a robust forecasting strategy during periods of instability. This approach is particularly well-suited for materials management, where short-term planning accuracy is critical to minimizing delays and maximizing throughput throughout a long chain of logistical and manufacturing operations that is all too common in the automotive industry.

Through this lens, the project integrates data analysis and model experimentation and real-time adaptability to Supply Chain Signals like supplier delays, transport lead times and inventory levels.

Sales forecasting is used for analyzing sales data and making accurate forecasts, allowing sales teams to better teams to better plan their sales efforts and make informed decisions thanks to the help of a variety of forecasting models.

For Key Performance Indicators (KPI) we'll take into consideration sales performance and forecast accuracy which help in measuring the amount of revenue generated from sales over a given period of time and helps in measuring the degree to which a sales forecast matches actual sales results.

For this project we will look into the sales data of a global company that handles B2B products and services, where external factors such as supply chain disruptions or significant economic changes, and seasonal trends will be considered.
